Kraken: A Tale of Revenge

Overview

Kraken: A Tale of Revenge is a turn-based RPG with fishing mechanics for combat in a steampunk water world.

Richard Pequod was a fisherman taking care of his daughter in a sleepy seaside town. One day out of nowhere the titular Kraken attacked, destroying all Richard held dear. Years later Richard is knocked out of his depression daze by rumors that the Kraken has resurfaced, spurring him to embark on his tale of revenge.

Concept Creation

The initial concept of Kraken was just one of many for a WarioWare style collection of minigames satirizing the prevalence of fishing minigames in the RPG/JRPG genres. My imagination quickly outgrew the scope of the project, so I homed in on the game scenario that had the most potential for fun and addicting gameplay while acting as a vehicle for seamless storytelling. With inspirations pulled from sources ranging from Moby Dick to Pok√©mon, the tale of the Kraken and the seafaring world where it takes place began to take shape.

System Design

The core gameplay loop of Kraken is explore -> battle-> repeat, with a secondary loop based on battle preparation. The story will push the player to travel to different parts of the world. As they explore random encounters and boss enemies will stifle their progress, but if they overcome that they can earn rewards that help prepare them for other battles. These two loops keep the player engaged by giving multiple short- and long-term goals.

Narrative Writing

Any RPG worth its salt has a story that keeps players invested over the game‚Äôs length. For Kraken I wanted to make a character that players could empathize with while still feeling like someone who has enough motivation for revenge. The game starts with Pequod losing everything that made him happy, giving players a chance to grow with him. The plan is to have Pequod go through trials that put him through the five stages of grief, ending with the defeat of the Kraken and his realization that he must continue living to honor the memory of his daughter
