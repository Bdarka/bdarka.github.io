Genre Mash-Up


Core Statement

Genre Mash-Up is a Fighter game where each character is based on a different video game genre. My main goals for this project are to learn how to make Fighter games and find ways to make that genre more accessible.



Game Pillars

Reimagined Mechanics: Every character is an adaptation of a specific game genre. Their attacks and systems are inspired by mechanics from the genre they represent. Any fan of said genre should be immediately familiar with that character representative.

Rewarding Combat: Combat decisions extend beyond “which of my attacks do the most damage?” Every move a character can do will have both global and situational uses. This incentivises mastery over whatever character the player picks.

Accessiblity: The Fighter game genre is notoriously difficult for new players. Execution is the first hurdle, so players can choose between two input styles depending on their proficiency. Onboarding is another roadblock, so the tutorial will tie jargon to tangible concepts.


Specific Contributions

Below are details of the specific designs I created for the project and my thought process behind them.

Mechanics Design

Early in the project I decided that each fighter should have mechanics tied to their genre inspirations. This was challenging for the Fashion character as I tried to balance the slow methodical process in that genre with the fast pace of fighter games. The solution was an outfit system that allowed the Fashion fighter to dress their opponent.. Each piece of clothing has one attribute: Preppy, Cool, and Cute. The opponent has three areas they can be clothed: Tops, Bottoms, and Shoes. Below is a table with the outcomes based on the pieces they’re wearing when the outfit is complete:

If all three clothing types are used in an outfit then they receive damage instead.

In Fashion games Preppy, Cool, and Cute are categories that all others are derived from. Keeping the amount of clothing types to 3 keeps the Outfit System simple enough to keep track of during a fight while still staying true to what inspired it.

The Outfit System effects were decided based on two factors: what types of effects would be useful but fair and would that effect make sense for that outfit combination. For instance the logic behind lowering the speed of an all Cool outfit is a reference for walking away slowly from explosions. Lowering the other player’s speed is also an incredibly handy effect. Each time the Fashion player makes a successful outfit their Outfit Lv goes up, changing the effect of the next set of clothes. Wardrobe Malfunctions lower the Outfit Lv in exchange for damage. This creates a risk and reward choice of upping the outfit level to get stronger effects or cashing out for more straightforward damage.


Character Design

Creating characters in Genre fighter is a multistep process. I start by asking the question “what is a popular video game genre not currently represented in the game?” Through research of classic genres it became clear that Genre Fighter needed a Shooter game inspired character.

Once I knew the genre to adapt, I studied recent and quintessential Shooter games. I took note of mechanics that were present throughout the genre’s history, like weapon selection and reloading. I represented these game designs in Gun Fire. Shooter players pick up ammo during a match, which they can load into one of 4 gun modes. Once they run out of ammo they can no longer use that move until they procure more. This makes Shooter follow the same logic as the games he represents, creating an entry point for new players.


Next Steps

My next milestone for this project is prototyping the rest of the attacks for Shooter before creating another character. Create permanent assets for Shooter character. Add music and title screen to make playtests feel more polished.
